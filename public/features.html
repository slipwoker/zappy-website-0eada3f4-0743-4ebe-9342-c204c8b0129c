Got Questions?

Common Questions, Answered

Everything you need to know about working together — from scope and timelines to what happens long after launch.

What does the scope of a typical project include?

Every engagement begins with a discovery phase where we map out goals, constraints, and deliverables together. A standard project scope covers research and strategy, design and prototyping, development, content integration, and a structured handoff. Whether you're building a focused catalog feature or a full-scale platform, the scope is defined collaboratively so there are no surprises — just a clear, shared roadmap from day one.

How long does a project typically take?

Timelines vary depending on complexity, but most projects fall into one of three bands: focused engagements (2–4 weeks), mid-scale builds (6–10 weeks), and comprehensive platform projects (12–20 weeks). We'll agree on a realistic schedule during scoping and share a milestone plan so you always know where things stand. Rush timelines can sometimes be accommodated — just let us know your constraints early.

How do we collaborate throughout the process?

Collaboration is built into every phase, not bolted on at the end. You'll receive regular progress updates, participate in structured review sessions at each milestone, and have direct access via your preferred communication channel — whether that's email, Slack, or scheduled calls. We keep feedback loops tight and documentation clear so you're always informed and never waiting on answers.

How many revisions are included, and how do they work?

Each project phase includes a defined number of revision rounds — typically two per major deliverable. Revisions are consolidated into clear feedback sessions so the process stays focused and efficient. If your needs evolve significantly mid-project, we'll discuss scope adjustments transparently rather than quietly absorbing extra work or surprising you with overages. Our goal is a result you're genuinely proud of.

What do I actually receive at the end of the project?

Deliverables are agreed upon during scoping and documented before work begins. Depending on the project, you might receive design files (Figma, exported assets), a deployed codebase with documentation, a content-ready catalog build, or a combination of all three. Every handoff includes a walkthrough session and written notes so your team can confidently take ownership from day one.

What happens after the project launches?

Launch is a milestone, not an ending. We offer a post-launch support window for bug fixes and minor adjustments at no extra charge. Beyond that, ongoing retainer arrangements are available for teams who want a consistent partner for updates, new features, or iterative improvements. We're invested in the long-term success of what we build together — not just in delivering and disappearing.
